20 names of unspecified gender, 10 masculine names and 10 feminine*, from the Annals:
    * As the creator of these scripts, I don't personally subscribe to names as being implicitly gendered. I could argue this by demonstrating that Maolíosa was orignally considered masculine as was Naoise but both are currently considered either feminine or gender neutral, but I do not subscribe to gender as an objective concept and I feel that warrants mentioning. I have a very very complex, nuanced, and personal understanding of what names are, in a more or less cosmological sense, and so while I personally might consider a name traditionally masculine or feminine, gender is a social construct and not an objective concept.
